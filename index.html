About me

I am a first-generation Mechanical Engineer from Khulna, Bangladesh, whose academic journey began in a madrasa-based education system before transitioning into formal engineering education. Rebuilding my foundation in mathematics and analytical reasoning developed a disciplined, self-directed approach to learning and research.

Growing up in a climate-vulnerable coastal region shaped my research interests in sustainable materials and energy-efficient engineering systems. I am particularly interested in the integration of materials characterization, computational modeling, and advanced manufacturing to address durability and environmental performance challenges.

I am motivated by research that connects fundamental engineering principles with practical, scalable impact—especially for regions facing environmental stress and industrial limitations. My goal is to contribute to rigorous, application-driven research that advances sustainable technological development.

Publications and Research Experience

  2. Curie Temperature Variation in Rare Earth Doped Spinel Ferrites: A Review (February 2025)

    Materials Science, Magnetism Published in JOM, Volume 77, Pages 2684–2697 (2025) Supervised by M. D. Hossain

    We conducted a structured review of over 100 peer-reviewed articles on the influence of rare earth (RE) elements as doping ions in spinel ferrites, focusing on the Curie temperature (TC), which determines the temperature at which a ferromagnetic material loses its permanent magnetization. We provided a comprehensive tabular analysis covering the chemical composition, RE concentration, sample preparation methods, sintering temperatures, and TC of the ferrites, along with graphs illustrating the relationship between TC and RE content. Our findings indicated that increasing the RE ratio in spinel ferrites generally leads to a reduction in the Curie temperature, and this review serves as a valuable resource for researchers seeking an overview of TC variation in RE-doped spinel ferrites. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s11837-024-07117-w
